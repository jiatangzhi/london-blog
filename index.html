Why London?

London, the city of diversity

London is renowned for being one of the most culturally diverse cities in the world. With a history spanning over two thousand years, London has seen people from all corners of the globe come and make it their home. As a result, London has become a melting pot of different cultures, languages, and traditions. You can find people from all over the world living in London, speaking different languages and celebrating their own customs and festivals. From Chinatown to Little India, from Brixton to Hackney, London is home to countless cultural enclaves, each with their own distinct character and flavour.

London's diversity is reflected not only in its people but also in its food, fashion, music, and art scenes. You can taste a variety of cuisines from all over the world in London, from street food to Michelin-starred restaurants. The fashion scene in London is renowned for its eclectic mix of styles, combining influences from all over the world. London is also a hub for music, with venues that showcase every type of music imaginable, from classical to hip-hop. Art lovers can explore galleries and museums that exhibit works from all over the world, reflecting the diverse cultural influences that have shaped London over the centuries.

My favourite restaurants in London

Bacchanalia

Bacchanalia Mayfair is a luxurious restaurant offering a contemporary interpretation of Italian cuisine with an extensive wine list.

Address:

1-3 Mount St

What I like about it

The ambiance is gorgeous to take pictures and I loved the Mediterrenean cuisine.

Book

Hakkasan Mayfair

Hakkasan Mayfair is a Michelin-starred restaurant serving contemporary Cantonese cuisine in a stylish and elegant setting.

Address:

17 Bruton St

What I like about it

The luxurious atmosphere and great place to come for a celebratory dinner if you fancy Asian food!

Book

The Ivy Canary Wharf

The Ivy Canary Wharf is an elegant and sophisticated restaurant with a contemporary British menu, located in the heart of London's business district.

Address:

50 Canada Square Park

What I like about it

The stunning views of the waterfront, combined with a delicious and diverse menu. You should try the afternoon tea!
